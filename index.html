About Us

Although its roots began in the marginalised communities of US, Hip Hop culture has been embraced by all who share in the values of positivity and courage, freedom, passion and creativity in shared artistic expression. This is what defines NUS Dance Blast and what Blast aims to promote to the NUS and beyond.

Set up in 2001, NUS Dance Blast! is the largest street dance group in NUS with more than 180 members. Each year, strict audition was held to see the best 80 freshmen chosen from around 200 auditionees. Led by Blastâ€™s Resident Choreographer Patrick Loo, a much celebrated exponent of hip-hop dance, Blast trains to develop their own distinct dance style. They have also trained with international choreographers from US, Japan and South Korea to extend their skills in a wider variety of dance styles. Beyond merely preparing for hip-hop performances, through intense training workshops, regular rehearsals and practices, the members of NUS Dance Blast learn the value of commitment, creativity and community.



Resident Choreographer



Patrick 'Tricky P' Loo

Patrick 'Tricky P' Loo

Patrick Loo belongs to the select group of choreographers where his influence and mark is most strongly felt in the Hip-Hop scene in Singapore. He began dancing late in life but quickly carved out a career in dance performance where he was one of the members of the now-defunct, Singapore Broadcasting Corporation's Performance and Dance Company, for thirteen years.

Since then, he has gone on to develop his natural gift of choreographing where his works include choreographing for major fashion shows (Nokia, Swaroski etc), various artists (Aaron Kwok, B.A.D etc) and concerts (Yellow Ribbon Project, Hoobastank etc). An established teacher of hip-hop, Patrick brings out the versatility and performance quality in dancers and is lauded for the success of his many teams that have gone on to win national dance competitions (Funkamania, Suntec etc) as well as Asian dance competitions (Big Five etc). A testament to his dedication as a teacher, Patrick founded all girl team 'Platinum', in 2000, which has since swept national dance competitions for years, got placed in Asian dance competitions and are now nationally recognized as an award-winning dance group. His signature hip-hop choreographic style is to always create innovative movements which are fused with tons of attitude and fun.
